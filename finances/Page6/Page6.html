Earnings Per Share

Introduction

Earnings per share (EPS) is calculated as a company's profit divided by the outstanding shares of its common stock. EPS indicates how much money a company makes for each share of its stock, and is a widely used metric to estimate corporate value.

Key Points

  • Earnings per share (EPS) is a company's net profit divided by the number of common shares it has outstanding.
  • EPS indicates how much money a company makes for each share of its stock, and is a widely used metric to estimate corporate value.
  • A higher EPS indicates greater value because investors will pay more for a company's shares if they think the company has higher profits relative to its share price.
  • EPS can be arrived at in several forms, such as excluding extraordinary items or discontinued operations, or on a diluted basis.

Earnings Per Share Formula

Earnings Per Share = (Net Income - Preferred Dividends)/(Common Shares Outstanding)

Price/Earnings Ratio = (Price Per Share)/(Earnings Per Share)
